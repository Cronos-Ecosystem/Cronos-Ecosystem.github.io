Bridging ETH to Linea involves interacting with a smart contract on Ethereum mainnet that locks your ETH and triggers a corresponding credit on Linea, a zkEVM-based Layer 2. This is not a simple wallet-to-wallet transfer. You are executing contract logic that must be verified.

Every signature you approve has real financial consequences. Treat the process as an operational procedure: verify domain, contract, network, and transaction details at each step. The explorer is the source of truth—not the bridge interface.

What transfer ETH from Ethereum to Linea means in practice

Definition: Transferring ETH from Ethereum to Linea means locking ETH in a bridge contract on Ethereum mainnet (L1), which triggers a message to Linea (L2) to credit your address with equivalent ETH representation.

Mental model: Lock on L1 → L1 confirmations → sequencer processes message → credit on L2 → verify on L2 explorer.

Many users assume bridging is instantaneous or identical to sending ETH between wallets. It is not. You are interacting with a bridge contract, and the L2 balance only updates after the L1 transaction is confirmed and processed.

  • Transaction hash: Unique identifier of your transaction.
  • Confirmations: Number of blocks mined after your transaction.
  • Block explorer: Independent interface to inspect on-chain data.
  • Chain ID: Unique identifier for a blockchain network.
  • RPC endpoint: Node URL your wallet uses to read/write blockchain data.
  • Approvals: Permissions granted to contracts to spend tokens.
  • Nonce: Sequential transaction counter for your address.
  • Gas limit: Maximum computational steps allowed for a transaction.
  • Base fee: Protocol-set minimum gas price per block.
  • Priority fee: Tip to incentivize validators.
On the explorer, confirm the contract address matches official documentation and review the transaction “Input Data” to ensure it corresponds to a bridge deposit function.

Common mistakes when move ETH to Linea network and fixes

Most errors stem from rushing or skipping verification steps.

Wrong network selected

User sends from a testnet instead of mainnet.

Fix: Confirm network at top of wallet before signing.

Insufficient gas

Transaction fails due to low gas limit.

Fix: Use wallet’s recommended gas settings.

Phishing site usage

User connects to cloned bridge site.

Fix: Verify contract address independently.

Clipboard malware

Address changes after copy-paste.

Fix: Compare first and last 6 characters manually.

Rushing confirmations

User expects instant L2 credit.

Fix: Wait for required L1 confirmations.

Ignoring explorer

Trusting UI status only.

Fix: Always confirm on explorer.

Unlimited approvals

Granting excessive permissions.

Fix: Revoke approvals post-bridge.

Manual gas misconfiguration

Setting extremely low priority fee.

Fix: Use wallet default unless advanced user.

Fake explorer

Checking transaction on malicious site.

Fix: Bookmark official explorer.

Large first transfer

Bridging full balance immediately.

Fix: Perform small test transaction first.
