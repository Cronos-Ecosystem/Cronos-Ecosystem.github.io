What Does “ETH to Linea” Actually Mean?

“ETH to Linea” refers to transferring Ether (ETH) from the Ethereum mainnet (Layer 1) to the Linea network, a Layer 2 scaling solution. Technically, your ETH is locked in a bridge contract on Ethereum and a corresponding representation becomes usable on Linea.

Linea is an Ethereum-compatible Layer 2 designed to improve scalability while maintaining compatibility with Ethereum smart contracts and tooling. When you bridge ETH to Linea, you are not sending ETH directly to a new blockchain address in the traditional sense. Instead, you interact with a canonical bridge contract that escrows funds and credits your address on Linea.

Mental Model

  • Ethereum mainnet = security anchor and settlement layer.
  • Bridge contract = escrow vault on Ethereum.
  • Linea network = execution layer with lower fees.
  • Your wallet address = same private key, different network context.
You can independently verify the bridge contract interaction on Ethereum by checking the transaction input data and the contract address against official documentation.

The most important concept is that security depends on the correctness of the bridge contract and your interaction with it. Always verify the contract address and the interface before approving transactions.

How to Bridge ETH to Linea (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Secure Your Wallet

Use a hardware wallet or a well-secured browser wallet with updated software. Ensure your seed phrase is offline and never pasted into any website.

Never input your seed phrase to “verify” a bridge. Legitimate bridges do not ask for private keys.
Confirm firmware and wallet extension are up to date before proceeding.

Step 2: Confirm Ethereum Mainnet

Switch your wallet network to Ethereum mainnet. Check that your ETH balance appears correctly and that you have enough for both the transfer and gas.

Verify network name and chain ID in your wallet match Ethereum mainnet.

Step 3: Access the Official Linea Bridge

Navigate directly to the official Linea bridge interface using a trusted source. Avoid links from unsolicited messages or ads.

Phishing sites often copy the bridge UI. Bookmark the official site after verifying it.
Cross-check the bridge contract address with official documentation before connecting.

Step 4: Connect Wallet

Click “Connect Wallet” and approve the connection request. Ensure the domain shown in your wallet matches the official bridge.

In your wallet popup, confirm the domain and permissions before approving.

Step 5: Enter ETH Amount

Input the amount of ETH to transfer. Leave enough ETH on Ethereum for gas fees. For first-time use, start with a small test amount.

Test with a small amount of ETH to Linea before bridging your full balance.

Step 6: Review Transaction Details

Carefully review gas fees, contract address, and transaction data. Confirm you are interacting with the correct bridge contract.

If gas fees look abnormally high, pause and verify network congestion.
Compare the contract address shown in your wallet with the official published address.

Step 7: Confirm and Wait for Finality

Approve the transaction in your wallet. Wait for sufficient Ethereum confirmations before expecting funds on Linea.

Track the transaction hash on an Ethereum explorer and confirm it is successful.

Step 8: Switch to Linea Network

After confirmation, switch your wallet network to Linea. If not already added, add Linea manually using official parameters.

Ensure the chain ID and RPC details match official Linea documentation.

Step 9: Verify ETH Balance on Linea

Check that your ETH balance appears on Linea. It should correspond to the bridged amount minus any applicable bridge fees.

Use a Linea block explorer to confirm your address balance and transaction history.

Security Checklist: Risks & Mitigation

Do

  • Verify bridge contract addresses.
  • Use hardware wallets for large transfers.
  • Test with small amounts first.
  • Bookmark official interfaces.
  • Monitor transaction confirmations.

Don’t

  • Click random bridge ads.
  • Approve unknown token permissions.
  • Ignore unusually high gas warnings.
  • Rush under time pressure.
  • Share private keys or seed phrases.
Bridge smart contracts are high-value targets. Phishing and contract spoofing are the most common attack vectors.

Common Mistakes When Moving ETH to Linea

Wrong Network

Sending funds while connected to the wrong chain.

Fix: Always confirm Ethereum mainnet before bridging.

Phishing Site

Using a fake bridge interface.

Fix: Verify domain and bookmark official site.

No Gas Left

Bridging entire ETH balance.

Fix: Leave extra ETH for gas.

Skipping Test Transfer

Bridging large amount first time.

Fix: Send a small test transaction first.

Incorrect RPC

Adding unofficial Linea RPC.

Fix: Copy parameters from official documentation only.

Ignoring Confirmations

Assuming instant finality.

Fix: Wait for sufficient confirmations.

Approval Misunderstanding

Approving unexpected token spending.

Fix: Read wallet prompts carefully.

Explorer Confusion

Checking wrong block explorer.

Fix: Use Ethereum explorer for deposit and Linea explorer for balance.
