What It Means to Bridge ETH to Linea

Bridging ETH to Linea means moving Ether from Ethereum mainnet to the Linea network, a Layer 2 built to scale Ethereum. You are not sending ETH directly to a new chain address in the traditional sense.

Instead, your ETH is locked in a smart contract on Ethereum and represented on Linea. This process requires a bridge because Ethereum mainnet and Linea maintain separate state environments.

  • Ethereum Mainnet: source chain where ETH is locked
  • Linea: Layer 2 where ETH is represented
  • Bridge contract: coordinates lock and release logic

If you simply send ETH to a Linea address from Ethereum without a bridge interface, the funds will not arrive as usable balance on Linea.

How the Ethereum → Linea Bridge Works

The canonical bridge uses smart contracts on Ethereum to lock your ETH. After confirmation, a corresponding representation is made available on Linea.

Deposits (Ethereum → Linea) typically complete after required Ethereum confirmations. Withdrawals (Linea → Ethereum) may involve additional verification steps before final release on mainnet.

  • Deposit: ETH locked on Ethereum, credited on Linea
  • Withdrawal: ETH burned/unlocked on Linea, released on Ethereum
  • Finality depends on source chain confirmations

The bridge UI may show “processing,” but the block explorer (Ethereum and Linea) is the source of truth.

Step-by-Step: How to Send ETH to Linea

Use the verified Linea bridge interface and connect a wallet such as MetaMask on Ethereum mainnet. Always confirm the URL before connecting.

Select Ethereum as the source network and Linea as the destination. Enter the ETH amount and review the estimated gas fee on Ethereum.

  2. Select Ethereum → Linea
  3. Enter ETH amount and review gas
  4. Confirm transaction in wallet
  5. Verify transaction hash on Etherscan
  6. Switch wallet to Linea and confirm balance

After confirmation on Ethereum, switch your wallet RPC to Linea and check the credited ETH balance using the Linea block explorer.

Fees & Timelines

When bridging ETH to Linea, you pay gas on the source chain (Ethereum) for deposits. Ethereum gas varies based on network congestion.

Withdrawals from Linea back to Ethereum may include Linea transaction fees plus Ethereum gas when finalizing on mainnet.

Always check both explorers to confirm completion rather than relying only on the bridge interface status.

Security Checklist Before You Bridge

Most losses occur due to phishing, fake bridge interfaces, or incorrect network selection. Treat every bridge interaction as high risk until verified.

  • Use the project’s official documentation to access the bridge
  • Double-check the domain and SSL certificate
  • Never share your seed phrase
  • Consider a hardware wallet for larger transfers
  • Review transaction details before signing

Do not approve unrelated token permissions during bridging. ETH deposits usually do not require ERC‑20 approvals.

Common Mistakes When Moving ETH to Linea

Sending ETH without using a bridge is a frequent error. Funds may not be credited correctly if the proper mechanism is bypassed.

  • Selecting the wrong network in wallet
  • Insufficient ETH for mainnet gas
  • Assuming withdrawal is instant
  • Trusting UI status without explorer verification

Network mismatch errors can often be avoided by confirming both the source (Ethereum) and destination (Linea) before submitting.

Troubleshooting Delays and Stuck Transactions

If your ETH to Linea transfer appears stuck, copy the transaction hash and check Etherscan. Confirm it has enough block confirmations.

Then check the Linea explorer for the corresponding credit event. UI delays can occur even when the on-chain state is correct.

  • Pending on Ethereum: consider gas replacement if appropriate
  • Confirmed on Ethereum but not on Linea: wait for bridge processing
  • No record on explorer: verify correct wallet and network

Contact official support only through verified channels listed in official documentation.

Alternatives to the Native Bridge

Some users choose third-party cross-chain bridges or centralized exchanges that support withdrawals to Linea. Each option has different trust assumptions.

  • Third-party bridges: faster UX, additional smart contract risk
  • Centralized exchanges: custodial risk, operational dependency
  • Canonical bridge: aligned with project design

Evaluate liquidity, security history, and documentation before using alternatives.
