ETH to lInea quick start workflow

Before you start

  • Confirm you are on Ethereum mainnet in your wallet before initiating any bridge transaction.
  • Bookmark the official Linea bridge and explorer domains manually; do not rely on ads or search results.
  • Verify the bridge smart contract address on the official explorer before signing.
  • Ensure you control the destination wallet private keys or hardware wallet.
  • Check current Ethereum gas conditions in your wallet before submitting.
  • Have enough ETH on mainnet to cover both the bridge amount and gas fees.
  • Understand that bridging is not an instant swap; it is a cross-chain contract interaction.
  • Disable suspicious browser extensions and confirm your device is malware-free.
  • Double-check the destination address character by character.
Start with a small test transfer before moving a larger amount.

Sanity checks after bridging

  • Locate your transaction hash (tx hash) in your wallet activity.
  • Confirm the transaction status is “Success” on the Ethereum explorer.
  • Wait for sufficient confirmations before expecting funds on Linea.
  • Switch your wallet network to Linea and verify ETH balance.
  • Check the Linea explorer for the credit transaction.
  • Ensure no unexpected token approvals were granted.
  • Record the tx hash for future reference.
If the explorer shows success and correct contract interaction, the bridge logic executed as intended.

Bridging ETH to Linea involves interacting with a smart contract on Ethereum mainnet that locks your funds and triggers a corresponding credit on Linea. This is real money moving across execution environments. Treat every click as irreversible until proven otherwise.

A verification-first mindset reduces risk. You should independently confirm contract addresses, transaction status, and balances using official explorers rather than trusting a website interface alone.
